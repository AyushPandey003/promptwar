# Design System: Herb & Hearth x SpiceRoute

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere
A calm, culinary interface with an eggshell canvas, herb-green actions, soft cards, rounded geometry, and light ambient depth. The feeling is organized, inviting, and practical for a home cook planning meals in a compact kitchen.

## 2. Color Palette & Roles
- **Herb Green (#334f2b):** primary actions, headings, strong navigation accents.
- **Sage Lift (#cae9bc):** active chips, positive status, selected navigation states.
- **Warm Eggshell (#fcf9f8):** base background and spacious page canvas.
- **Mist Surface (#f6f3f2 / #f0eded):** soft secondary panels and utility areas.
- **Kitchen Charcoal (#1b1c1c / #434840):** high-legibility text and supporting content.
- **Muted Outline (#c3c8bd / #73796f):** subtle borders, separators, and structured controls.

## 3. Typography Rules
Use a clean modern sans with strong contrast between bold editorial headings and highly readable body copy. Headlines feel confident and slightly compact; body text stays airy for grocery lists, substitutions, and cooking steps.

## 4. Component Stylings
- **Buttons:** rounded or pill-shaped, herb-green primary fill, high-contrast white text.
- **Cards:** bright white or soft-mist surfaces with generous rounding and whisper-soft shadows.
- **Inputs/Forms:** soft backgrounds, muted sage borders, stronger herb-green focus states.
- **Status Chips:** pill-shaped, color-coded, compact, and glanceable.
- **Navigation:** friendly rounded pills with gentle hover treatment and clear active state.

## 5. Layout Principles
Use roomy spacing, a dashboard-style grid, and modular content cards. Keep the planner central, groceries and saved context adjacent, and supporting details in lighter side panels. The UI should feel breathable and kitchen-friendly, not dense or enterprise-heavy.
